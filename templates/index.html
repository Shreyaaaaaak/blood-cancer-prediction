What is ALL?
Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia (ALL) is a type of blood cancer that starts in the bone marrow, where blood cells are made. It causes the body to produce too many immature white blood cells called lymphoblasts, which crowd out healthy blood cells. ALL is the most common cancer in children but can also affect adults. It progresses quickly and requires immediate medical attention. Early detection significantly improves the chances of successful treatment.
